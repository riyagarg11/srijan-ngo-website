Together We Are Changing Lives

India's first FPO incorporated under cooperative societies act 1965

About Us

  • India's First farmer producer organisation(FPO Under 10,000 scheme) under the ambit of the cooperative societies act has been registered
    in Varanasi,Prime Minister Narendra Modi's parliamentry constituency in Uttar Pradesh
  • The FPO incorporated under the guidance of NCDC and Horticulture Department

    • Success Story

    Journey of FPO starts on 25TH February 2021


  • Turnover of FPO -

    • Financial year 2021-22 FPO turnover is 60 lakhs
    • Financial year 2022-23 the turnover reacher 1.4cr

  • Assets of FPO -

    • 750 registered farmers
    • Training centre for farmers
    • Mushroom spawn/seed lab
    • Cold chain system
    • Milk collection centre
    • Small processing centre
    • Kolhu for extracting cold press oil
    • Crop advisory to our farmers through various institute scientists (BHU,ICAR-IIVR,KVK etc.)
    • New generation agriculture based drones for member farmers


Our work areas

  • Mushroom Productions

    • Hundreds of female farmers in Self Help Groups get trained to grow Mushroom(Oyester) inside their homes and earn to uplift their livelihood and nutritional security



  • Processing and Exports

    1. NCDC have provided coldroom of 30mt capacity from which we are able to do all processing and epxort related activities
    2. Procurement of fresh vegetable in coldrooms for long lastening freshness by which farmers get good prices of their produce in local or big markets
    3. Processing of Green Chillies, Mushrooms, Peas, Mustard, Flex Seeds etc. by which farmers double their income
    4. Recently we exported fresh vegetables of our FPO members to UAE so they get good rates of their produce in international markets


  • Milk collection and distribution

    1. FPO members get the benefits of milk marketing at very good market rates
    2. FPO facilates the farmers to raise their dairy business by the milk collection centre setup

Our Efforts

  • Problems faced by the farmers

    1. The limited production quantities
    2. Lack of farmers' access to public resources, quality inputs, credit facility, modern technologies etc.
    3. Frequent crop failures, Lack of assured market, income saftey and poorly developed supply chain has resulted in high dependecy of farmers on the exploitative intermediaries and local money lenders
    4. Lack of awareness of the government schemes
    5. Limited access in government organisationS to avail the benefits of agriculture sector
    6. Lack of Knowledge about the selection/quality parameters of crops which gives good price in markets

  • Collective efforts of FPO for their members

    1. 750 member farmers till now get benefits from the FPO by various means like Quality seeds from ICAR-INDIAN Institute of Vegetable Research,Varanasi
    2. Crop advisory from various agriculture scientists of reputated institutions like B.H.U,IIVR,KVK etc.
    3. Get easy access in markets at good rates via FPO
    4. Female farmers get income through mushroom cultivation at their homes
    5. Training programs on various agriculture and allied sector conducted for our member farmers to double their income
    6. Dairy farmers get good price up to Rs.98/Litre, Which was earlier Rs.30/Litre
